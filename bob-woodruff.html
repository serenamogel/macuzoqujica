Bob Woodruff ABC News and Reporting Career

In 1996, Woodruff joined ABC News, and from that point forward, he became a familiar face on television screens across the nation. His coverage spanned critical issues both domestically and globally. In December 2005, he took the helm as the host of “ABC World News Tonight,” succeeding Peter Jennings.

However, fate dealt a challenging hand to Woodruff on January 29, 2006, when he suffered a traumatic injury from an IED explosion while reporting on U.S. and Iraqi security forces. This incident forced him off the air temporarily, but it didn’t break his spirit.

Remarkably, Woodruff returned to ABC News in February 2007, just over a year after the life-altering incident. His first on-air broadcast, “To Iraq and Back: Bob Woodruff Reports,” provided a poignant look into his journey of recovery and highlighted the struggles faced by service members returning with similar disabilities.

Bob Woodruff Global Reporting and Recognition

Since his return, Woodruff has continued to make a global impact. From investigating nuclear threats in North Korea to serving as ABC’s principal correspondent in Asia, particularly China, his journalistic prowess knows no bounds. His critically acclaimed documentary, “China Inside Out,” delved into the implications of China’s rise on the world stage.

Woodruff’s extensive coverage has earned him prestigious awards, including the Alfred I. duPont Award and the George Foster Peabody Award, recognizing his outstanding contributions to broadcast journalism.

Bob Woodruff Personal Life and Family

Beyond the screen, Woodruff is a family man. Born to Frances Ann and Robert Norman Woodruff Jr., both real estate agents, family has always been a pillar of support in his life. In 1988, he married Lee McConaughy, and together they have four children – Macklin Robert, Cathryn, and twins Claire and Nora.

Challenges and Triumphs: “In an Instant”

In February 2007, Woodruff and his wife, Lee, co-authored the best-selling memoir “In an Instant.” The book provides a raw and honest account of his injuries in Iraq and the family’s journey in overcoming adversity and uncertainty. In the wake of his experiences, the Bob Woodruff Foundation (BWF) was founded to raise funds for injured service personnel, veterans, and their families.
