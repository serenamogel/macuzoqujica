Career

Christina used to perform in musical theater as a youngster. On her 21st birthday, she relocated to Los Angeles. She married the same year and started making music videos for a job, but the marriage didn’t last long. So, 18 months later, she divorced and moved into a modest apartment on her own to concentrate only on music. After a broken relationship, she relocated to Philadelphia and penned “Jar of Hearts” while missing her home and parents.

On June 30, 2010, her debut song “Jar of Hearts” was included in ‘So You Think You Can Dance,’ a reality program, in a performance by Billy Bell and Kathryn McCormick, which helped to establish her career in the music business. The song was such a smash success that it sold 100,000 copies in a week and peaked at No.17 on the Hot 100. On July 21, 2010, she inked a contract with Atlantic Records.

Christina released her first album, Lovestrong, on May 10, 2011, and it debuted at number four on the Billboard 200, selling 58,000 copies in its first week. In 2014, she launched her second album, Head or Heart Tour. From the film The Twilight Saga, ‘A Thousand Years’ from her album Lovestrong is one of her greatest singles to date, reaching No. 31 on the Billboard Hot 100. On May 14, 2013, Perri got the BMI award for her song “A Thousand Years” at the 61st annual BMI Pop Awards in Beverly Hills.
