Marty Lagina: Charity

Marty Lagina has established a charitable foundation named Marty Lagina Foundation to provide health care emergencies, food, and water to over 1 billion people worldwide.

Apart from that, Marty, along with The Discovery Network’s Curse of Oak Island, auctioned off some treasure items supporting South Shore Health Services Foundations (SSHSF).

They organized the second edition of the auction “Oak Island Shares the Dream” in 2020, with the first auction raising $19,000.

The fundraiser was the idea of geologist Terry Matheson who frequently appears on the show.

The other half of the proceeds went to Colchester East Hants Hospice Society.

Similarly, the “Oak Island Shares The Dream: Take 2” auction was launched on 30th September 2020 and ran through to 3rd October at 9 p.m.

Bidders at the auction sought after “Money Pit Core Sample,” a tube of soil dug up in search of Oak Island’s treasure.

The online auction was complemented by a Facebook Live stream from the Oak Island page at 7 pm on 3rd October.

Moreover, the Oak Island samples from the 2017 and 2018 digs were all signed by Marty & Rick Lagina, Dave Blankenship, and Craig Tester.

Investments

Apart from all his television productions, Marty is a very successful businessman. He is a known face in the wind power extraction and natural gas sector and bagged decent money from it.

During the 1990s, Terra Energy, one of his former companies, was a pioneer in extracting natural gas from shale around the mid-west.

However, Marty sold Terra Energy to CMS Energy for a whopping $58 million.

Since he had expertise in the natural gas and wind power field, he launched a new company called Heritage Sustainable which focuses mostly on wind power.

Marty plans to build 60 wind turbines in Missaukee, Michigan, which will make Marty the largest wind-energy producer in the whole state of Michigan.

That will also make him one of the largest overall wind-energy producers in the entire midwest.

Similarly, he envisions powering 25% of Michigan by wind and also replicates the process across the country.

Apart from the renewable energy business Marty also owns a winery called Mari Vineyards.

He launched the winery in 1999, which was not successful initially, but by 2006 they were producing a premium bottle called Row 7.

The vineyard spans 60 acres inland and is managed by Marty’s son Alex Lagina.

Marty Lagina: Career

Marty first worked as a petroleum engineer for Amoco Production Company.

At the time, he was also doing free-lancing consulting engineering works for several Michigan companies while also attending law school in 1979.

Afterward, he established his own wind power turbine and natural gas businesses.

Following that, he also produced and starred in the reality TV series The Curse of Oak Island, which premiered in 2014.

Prometheus Entertainment also supported them for the show. The Lagina brothers started the show, which became a fan favorite in no time.

The show’s main plot is to find the treasure that is supposedly buried on the Island. Moreover, it follows Marty and Rick, originally from Kingsford, Michigan, as they embark on the treasure hunting adventure.

Marty and Rick first developed an interest in the Island after reading a copy of Reader’s Digest article in 1965, which featured the history of a family by the name of Restall that at one point was investigating the long-lost supposed treasure.

Marty and his brother currently own a controlling interest in the Oak Island Tours Company, which is also the majority owner of the Island.

It’s been said that the treasure on the Island was left by North African gold merchants dating back to the 1300s.

However, it is just one of the theories regarding the original source of the treasure.
