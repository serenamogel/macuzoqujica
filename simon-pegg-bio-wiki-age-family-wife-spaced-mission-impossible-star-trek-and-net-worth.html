Simon Pegg Biography

Simon John Pegg, known professionally as Simon Pegg, is an English actor, comedian, screenwriter, and producer. He got famous in Britain as the co-creator of the Channel 4 sitcom Spaced, directed by Edgar Wright. He also co-wrote and starred in the Three Flavours Cornetto film trilogy: Shaun of the Dead of 2004, Hot Fuzz of 2007, and The World’s End of 2013.

Simon portrays Benji Dunn in the Mission: Impossible film series of 2006–present and he also played Montgomery Scott in Star Trek in 2009, Star Trek Into Darkness in 2013, and Star Trek Beyond in 2016, co-writing the latter. He and Nick Frost wrote and starred in the sci-fi film Paul of 2011.

Simon Pegg Career

Between 1998 and 2004, Simon has regularly featured on BBC Radio 4’s The 99p Challenge. His early appearances in TV series and movies include Asylum, Six Pairs of Pants, Faith in the Future, Big Train, and Hippies.

His other credits include appearances in the World War II mini-series Band of Brothers; the television comedies Black Books, Brass Eye and I’m Alan Partridge; and the movies The Parole Officer, 24 Hour Party People, and Guest House Paradiso.

Simon created and co-wrote the Channel 4 sitcom Spaced with Jessica Stevenson and directed by Edgar Wright, with whom Pegg and Stevenson had previously worked on Asylum in 1999. He was nominated for a British Comedy Award as Best Male Comedy Newcomer for his performance in the series.

In 2004, he starred in a spin-off of the television show Danger! 50,000 Volts! called Danger! 50,000 Zombies!, in which he played a zombie hunter named Dr. Fell. In a series of Big Finish Productions audio plays, Simon played mutant bounty hunter Johnny Alpha, the Strontium Dog, based on the character from British comic 2000 AD.

Simon also appeared in Big Finish Productions’ Doctor Who audio story Invaders From Mars as Don Chaney, and appeared playing the Editor in the Doctor Who television series, in the 2005 episode “The Long Game”. He also narrated the first series of the “making-of” documentary series Doctor Who Confidential.

Simon appeared in the film Mission: Impossible III, as Benji Dunn, an IMF technician who assists Tom Cruise‘s character, Ethan Hunt. He repeated the role in the sequel films Mission: Impossible Ghost Protocol in 2011, Mission: Impossible Rogue Nation in 2015, and Mission: Impossible Fallout in 2018.

Simon played Gus in Big Nothing together with David Schwimmer in 2006. That same year, he and Wright completed their second film, Hot Fuzz, which was released in February 2007. It’s about a police-action movie homage in which Pegg plays Nicholas Angel, a London policeman transferred to rural Sandford, a fictional village where grisly events take place. It also stars Nick Frost,

In 2007, he starred in The Good Night (directed by Jake Paltrow) and Run Fat boy Run directed by David Schwimmer. Additionally, he composed the dialogue for an English language re-release of the cult 2006 vivified Norwegian film, Free Jimmy in 2008.

He co-wrote the script for a film called Paul, about two young men who encounter a comedic extraterrestrial alien during a road trip across the US. The finished content appeared on the 2008 “Brit List”, a film-industry-ordered review of the best unproduced British screenplays, inspired by the American Black List.

In the eleventh film in the Star Trek film series, released on 8th May 2009, he played engineer Montgomery “Scotty” Scott in Star Trek, He repeated the role in the 2013 film Star Trek Into Darkness and Star Trek Beyond in 2016.

Simon appeared as William Burke in Burke and Hare, a film directed by John Landis about two Ulstermen who were notorious murderers and body snatchers in early 19th-century Edinburgh. He and Wright completed the Three Flavours Cornetto trilogy (the first two films being Shaun of the Dead and Hot Fuzz) with their 2013 film The World’s End.
